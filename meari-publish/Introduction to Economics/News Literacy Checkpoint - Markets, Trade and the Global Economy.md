## Quick Summary
Use this checkpoint to test whether market, trade, currency, debt, and crisis headlines now fit together. The questions ask you to decode stock indexes, bond yields, exchange rates, trade deficits, tariffs, government debt, IMF bailouts, and contagion without confusing financial-market language with the real economy underneath it.

This checkpoint checks whether you can read market and global-economy headlines without getting tripped up. You'll interpret what a stock index actually measures, what a "crash" does and doesn't signal about the real economy, and the inverse relationship between bond prices and yields that confuses so many readers.

From there you'll tackle the international side of the news: what a "strong dollar" means for travelers and importers, what a trade deficit really is, why government debt isn't the same as a household credit card, how tariffs and trade wars ripple through prices, and how financial contagion jumps across borders. Answer each question and review the explanation to sharpen your instincts.
