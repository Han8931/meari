## Quick Summary
Use this checkpoint to test whether central-bank, inflation, and recession headlines now feel readable. The questions focus on common news terms—basis points, hawkish and dovish signals, core inflation, real wages, technical recessions, and inverted yield curves—and ask you to connect each term to the economic mechanism behind it.

This checkpoint tests whether you can decode the central-bank and inflation language that fills the financial news. You'll work through what a "basis point" really means, how to read hawkish versus dovish signals, what a rate hike does to your mortgage and savings, and the crucial difference between headline and core inflation.

The second half focuses on recognizing recession and price-trend vocabulary: disinflation versus deflation, real versus nominal wages, the "two negative quarters" rule of thumb, and why an inverted yield curve makes economists nervous.
