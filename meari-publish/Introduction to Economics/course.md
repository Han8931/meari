A beginner's course in economics built for **economic news literacy**: its goal is that, by the end, you can pick up a newspaper or watch the news and actually understand what economic headlines mean — interest-rate decisions, inflation reports, GDP figures, market swings, trade disputes, and debt crises around the world. The first five modules build the core ideas; the final module, *Reading the Economic News*, turns them on real headlines.

## Quick Summary
This course teaches the basic economic toolkit for reading everyday news. You start with scarcity, trade-offs, incentives, demand, supply, and market equilibrium, then use those ideas to understand firms, inflation, unemployment, GDP, interest rates, budgets, trade, and market failures. The final module turns the concepts into practice: decoding central-bank statements, cost-of-living reports, recession signals, market moves, exchange rates, debt stories, and global crises.

By the end, you should be able to ask the right first questions when a headline appears: Which curve moved? Is this nominal or real? Who gains, who loses, and what trade-off is being hidden? Is the story about prices, output, jobs, credit, incentives, or international flows?
